Vestibular rehabilitation is a form of therapy that helps individuals suffering from vestibular disorders. It can help people who have suffered an injury, (as well as) those with chronic conditions such as Meniere's disease. The goal of this type of rehab is to reduce dizziness and balance problems caused by the disorder. It does this through exercises designed to retrain the brain and improve how it interprets information from the inner ear.

Moreover, vestibular rehab also aims to strengthen muscles used for balance and coordination, aswell as improving vision and strength in the neck muscles which are important for maintaining balance. It may involve practising activities such as walking on uneven surfaces or standing on one leg. Other treatments may include biofeedback or electrical stimulation - both of which can help alleviate symptoms! Besides these physical therapies, there are also techniques that enable people to manage their condition better, including relaxation exercises and habituation techniques.

Finally, vestibular rehab can be beneficial for anyone experiencing vertigo or other balance issues due to a vestibular disorder; however it is important to note that results vary from person-to-person. Therefore, it's best to consult your doctor before embarking upon any form of treatment plan. Ultimately though, with dedication and consistency you can expect improved balance and reduced dizzyness!

What is vestibular rehabilitation?

Vestibular rehabilitation is a form of physical therapy that focuses on improving symptoms associated with disorders of the inner ear and balance system.

How does vestibular rehabilitation help?

Vestibular rehabilitation helps to reduce dizziness, vertigo and other balance-related symptoms by exercising the muscles of the face, neck and eyes to improve balance and coordination.

What conditions can be treated with vestibular rehabilitation?

Vestibular rehabilitation can be used to treat a variety of conditions such as benign paroxysmal positional vertigo (BPPV), labyrinthitis, Meniere's disease, concussion or head trauma, and peripheral neuropathy.

Where can I find a physiotherapist in Cork who offers vestibular rehabilitation services?

There are several physiotherapists in Cork who offer vestibular rehabilitation services. It is recommended to check with your local health provider for recommendations or search online for specialist practitioners in your area.
